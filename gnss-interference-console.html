Connecting real data

This prototype runs the full detection engine on representative data modeled on real mid-2026 hotspots. Here is exactly how to feed it live signals, easiest first.

  1. GPSJam daily dataset (start here). John Wiseman publishes a daily H3-hexagon feed of "% bad aircraft" per cell under CC-BY. Drop it into loadCells() in place of the synthetic generator — the scoring, thresholds and feed all keep working. Daily resolution, zero auth.
  2. OpenSky live positions. Free for non-commercial use (OAuth2 client-credentials since Mar 2026). Gives you live aircraft positions to bin into cells — but not the integrity field on its own.
  3. Decode integrity yourself with pyModeS. Pull NIC / NACp straight out of raw ADS-B messages at the bit level. This is the "I went deeper" chapter — it proves you understand the protocol, not just the API.
  4. ADS-B Exchange. Exposes raw integrity fields (it's what GPSJam is built on), but live API access has moved toward paid tiers — check current terms.

The classification logic — jamming vs spoofing — is identical whichever source you wire in. That logic is the part that transfers to the job.
